You're Richer Than You Think.

Business Systems Analyst

During the four month co-op term I had at Scotiabank in the summer of 2017, I was assigned to one of the labs under the Payments and Technology department. The project underway was to replace an existing legacy system by implementing a more efficient file transferring system that incorporated track and trace. The new system would incorporate all existing functionalities in the legacy system and provide clients with a more reliable payment processing service. Below is a list of things I did at Scotiabank:

Worked with a cross functional team consisting of developers, quality assurance testers, business system analysts & solution architects in an agile environment that utilizes Scrum and Kanban.

Analyzed the source code for portions of the legacy system's functionality so that all existing behaviours are incorporated into the new integrated system requirements. Retrieved a set of seed data from the legacy system by sending test files to the corresponding server, while also creating a set of trivial test scenarios as a foundation for quality assurance testers to utilize for each system requirement. Obtained all the unique variations in the data input file names that consisted of a combiniation of scripts, strings & literals, so that testing covered all possible combinations.

Evaluated system requirements & gathered information to create user story definitions & acceptance criterias. Provided references for all user stories by articulating & maintaining documentation on Confluence & Jira. Participated in story mapping sessions & helped create physical & digital story maps.

Learned the architecture of the bank's various interconnected systems being used to onboard clients, and provide them access to transfer payment files to the bank so that the transactions within the payment file can be validated & processed for money release. Also learned the end to end flow of a payment file being passed into the primary systems that are apart of the interconnected system. Using this information learned, sequence diagrams were created for individual systems and a portion of their functionalities.

Future Leader's Challenge

The objective of this competition was to challenge one of the bank's internal or external processes by figuring out how it can be improved to increase efficiency/productivity and/or marketability depending on what processes are being challenged. Based on statistical evidence gathered through conducting surveys among our labs, we observed that majority of the employees felt that the bank took way too long to onboard them and provide them the necessities to start working. As a team of four we called our self 'Breaking Bank' and decided to break down and improve the internal onboarding process. Through hours of research and meetings with department leaders involved in the onboarding process, we consolidated our information into a professional and informative PowerPoint that was submitted.

Warehousing Associate

In the summer of 2015, and 2016 I worked at a company called Array Canada as a warehousing associate. Array Canada is a company that sells cosmetic displays & fixtures. This job was not focused on building technical skills but rather it introduced me to the work force and enhanced my transferable skills. In 2015 as a high school graduate, I worked alongside the team co-ordinator, department team and helped out with packaging, assembly & product refining. The department team consisted of over 30 employees with various occupations such as CNC machine operators, assembly line workers, welders and much more. In 2016, I was asked to work in the same department by the department leader because of the exceptional work I displayed in the previous summer. I worked in the same position as before but was put in charge of the packaging team which consisted of only 5 people. Here is a list of things that I did/learned at my time at Array:
